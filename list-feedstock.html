Quality & logistics

These fields help producers assess pyrolysis compatibility before contacting you.

Describe size in mm where possible. Producers use this to assess kiln compatibility.

There are four separate contamination risks producers care about. Answer each one honestly — misrepresentation can result in rejected loads, kiln damage, and listing removal.

1. Physical debris risk
Rocks, wire, metal, plastic that can damage pyrolysis equipment. Verifiable by: visual inspection at delivery or weight ticket discrepancy.
Score 1 = machine-sorted, no debris. Score 5 = unsorted, mixed material with visible debris.
2. Soil / ash content
Soil mixed into the material reduces char yield and carbon content. Verifiable by: ASTM E1755 ash content test (~$40 at A&L Western or Waypoint Analytical). Ask specifically for ash content on a dry basis.
Score 1 = freshly harvested, never ground-contacted. Score 5 = heavily contaminated, requires screening.
3. Chemical contamination risk
Treated wood, pesticides, heavy metals that make output biochar unsafe for food crops. Verifiable by: EPA Method 3051A heavy metals scan (~$80–150). Ask your lab for the IBI-recommended metals panel.
Score 1 = single-source agricultural material, no treatments known. Score 5 = mixed urban/industrial source, unknown history.
4. Other contamination risk
Any other contamination not covered above (e.g. mixed plastics, unknown additives, off-farm sources). Verifiable by: visual inspection and supplier documentation.
Score 1 = clean, well-documented source. Score 5 = mixed sources with unknown handling history.
