What certifications mean

Certifications tell you how a biochar product has been tested and verified. Here's what each one means for your farm.

OMRI Listed

Organic Materials Review Institute. Products with this mark are approved for use in certified organic production under the USDA National Organic Program. If your farm carries organic certification, this is required for any soil amendment you purchase.

IBI Certified

International Biochar Initiative. Third-party lab verified against standardized quality thresholds covering carbon content, heavy metals, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), and pH. The most rigorous independent biochar standard globally.

California Organic

CDFA-registered for use on California certified organic operations. In some categories stricter than federal OMRI requirements. Relevant if you are operating under California Certified Organic Farmers (CCOF) or similar state program.

✓ Lab Verified

The producer has submitted third-party lab results and we have reviewed them. Scorecard values shown on the listing are verified rather than self-reported. Always look for this indicator — it is the single most important trust signal on any listing.
