Biomass Explainer

What is an aggregator?

In this marketplace, an aggregator helps turn fragmented biomass into supply that buyers and processors can actually schedule, haul, and use.

Consolidates smaller sources

Aggregators combine material from multiple smaller suppliers into larger, workable loads so buyers do not need to coordinate many separate pickups.

Coordinates supply

They help align timing, trucking, storage, and handling so scattered biomass can move through the market more predictably.

Connects sellers to processors

When a seller does not yet have the scale or operational setup for a direct listing, an aggregator can help bridge that gap.
